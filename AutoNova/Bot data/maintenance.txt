Tesla recommends rotating tires every 10,000 to 12,000 miles to ensure even wear. Check tire pressure monthly and before long trips.

The 12V battery typically lasts 3–4 years. When you see a "Service Battery" warning, it may require replacement.

Tesla vehicles do not require traditional oil changes. However, brake fluid should be checked every 2 years.

Cabin air filters should be replaced every 2 years for Model 3/Y, and every 3 years for Model S/X.

Annual inspections are advised to check HVAC, power steering, and suspension components.

Software updates improve vehicle performance and fix known bugs. Connect your Tesla to Wi-Fi regularly.

Winter maintenance tip: Keep battery charged above 20% and precondition before driving in cold weather.

Minimal Scheduled Maintenance: Tesla vehicles have fewer moving parts compared to traditional gas cars, resulting in lower maintenance needs and longer service intervals.

Brake System Maintenance: Thanks to regenerative braking, Tesla vehicles experience less brake wear. Brake fluid levels are checked and replaced only as needed.

Tire Rotation and Balancing: Tesla recommends tire rotation every 10,000 to 12,000 miles or if uneven wear is detected, to ensure optimal range and safety.

Battery Maintenance: Tesla batteries are designed to last hundreds of thousands of miles. No regular battery service is required, but software monitors battery health continuously.

Software Diagnostics: Tesla vehicles run real-time diagnostics and can alert users or Tesla Service when issues are detected, often before they become serious problems.

Over-the-Air Fixes: Many minor bugs and performance issues can be resolved via software updates without a service center visit.

Mobile Service: Tesla’s Mobile Service units can come to your home or workplace for minor repairs, tire services, and inspections.

Service Alerts: The Tesla app provides service notifications, maintenance reminders, and remote diagnostic results.

Coolant Replacement: The battery coolant system typically requires replacement only every 4 years for Model S and X, and every 5 years for Model 3 and Y.

Air Conditioning Service: Cabin air filters should be replaced every 2 to 3 years, and AC service includes desiccant bag replacement to keep the system efficient.

Wiper Blade Replacement: Tesla recommends replacing wiper blades as needed, and the system alerts drivers when visibility is affected.

12V Battery Replacement: The auxiliary 12V battery typically lasts 3-5 years. Tesla vehicles notify users when replacement is due.

In-App Service Scheduling: Users can schedule service appointments through the Tesla app, including selecting specific issues and uploading photos or videos.

Digital Owner’s Manual: Tesla provides an in-car digital manual with maintenance guidelines, troubleshooting tips, and step-by-step guides.

Remote Diagnostics: Tesla Service can access vehicle data remotely to assess issues before scheduling or dispatching mobile service.

Low Fluid Notifications: Tesla vehicles alert the driver when windshield washer fluid is low or coolant needs inspection.

Rust Protection: Tesla uses rust-resistant materials and provides a limited warranty for body corrosion.

Alignment Checks: Recommended after significant pothole impact or irregular tire wear. Tesla Service can perform precision alignments if needed.

No Oil Changes: Since Tesla vehicles are electric, they do not require oil changes, fuel filter replacements, or emissions checks.

Drive Unit Maintenance: Tesla’s electric motors (drive units) require no regular service and are covered under an 8-year limited warranty in most models.

Remote Reset and Recovery: In case of minor software glitches, many issues can be resolved by performing a soft or hard reset using the touchscreen system.

Service History Access: Tesla maintains digital service records that are accessible through the owner’s Tesla account and mobile app.
